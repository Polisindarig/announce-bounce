§ 01 — Strategy

Four rules.
Two directions.
Spot only.

↗ Buy · MEXC

First Binance ecosystem entry

When Binance publishes the first listing-related announcement for a coin — spot, futures, Hodler airdrop, Launchpool or Launchpad — the bot buys the coin on MEXC where it already trades. Calibrated exit: TP +25% / SL −8% / 1h time stop.

↘ Sell · Binance

Delisting announcement

"Will Delist X" triggers an immediate market sell on any open position. OOS mean drop is −7.75% at five minutes and −17.30% by eight hours (p < 0.001). Acting at the next bar avoids the deep tail.

↘ Sell · Binance

Monitoring tag added

Binance's documented precursor to delisting. The short-term reaction is diffuse — −1.27% at five minutes — but the drift compounds to −5.56% over 24h. Median lead time to actual delisting in the OOS sample: 42 days.

— Skip

Everything else

Maintenance windows, regulatory notes, partnership integrations, promotional campaigns. None produce a tradeable edge after rigorous in-sample testing. The bot logs them and moves on.

§ 03 — Risk

No leverage.
No discretion.
Hard limits.

01 Position sizing

Fixed $1,000 per trade on a $10,000 balance — 10% of equity, roughly one-eighth of the full-Kelly fraction implied by the OOS edge. One symbol, one trade.

02 Stop-loss

Hard SL at −8% from entry, selected by a 1,408-variant in-sample grid search. Calibration frozen across the OOS window.

03 Time stop

Forced close at 1h. Listing momentum decays fast — Empirica (2024) shows post-listing tokens lose 6.34% in week one.

04 Concurrency

Maximum 3 open positions, one per symbol. No portfolio drift, no compounding correlation risk.

05 Cost modeling

Every entry pays a 1% slippage proxy plus a 10 bps round-trip taker fee. All reported returns are net of both.

06 Spot only · paper

No futures, no leverage. Currently running in paper mode; live deployment is out of scope for the thesis.
